# International Development Benchmarks

This dataset provides comparative analysis and benchmarks from successful technological transformation cases that inform Peru's 2040 strategy.

## Rapid Economic Transformation Examples

### South Korea (1960-1990): "Miracle on the Han River"

#### Key Metrics
| Indicator | 1960 | 1970 | 1980 | 1990 | 30-Year Change |
|-----------|------|------|------|------|----------------|
| GDP per Capita | $158 | $279 | $1,778 | $6,642 | +4,104% |
| Technology GDP Share | 5% | 12% | 25% | 35% | +30 percentage points |
| Manufacturing Share | 15% | 22% | 30% | 28% | +13 percentage points |
| Education Spending (% GDP) | 2.5% | 3.8% | 5.2% | 6.8% | +4.3 percentage points |
| R&D Spending (% GDP) | 0.2% | 0.4% | 0.8% | 1.8% | +1.6 percentage points |

#### Success Factors
- **State-Led Development**: Government coordination of industrial development
- **Education Investment**: Massive investment in technical and higher education
- **Export Orientation**: Focus on global markets from early stages
- **Technology Transfer**: Strategic partnerships with US, Japan, Germany
- **Long-term Planning**: Consistent 5-year development plans

#### Lessons for Peru
- Constitutional protection enabled long-term planning consistency
- Heavy state investment in infrastructure preceded private sector growth
- Technology transfer partnerships accelerated capability development
- Export markets essential for achieving scale and competitiveness

#### Strategic Approaches
- **Whole-of-Government**: Coordinated approach across all ministries
- **Pragmatic Governance**: Evidence-based policy making
- **Human Capital**: Aggressive talent attraction and development

#### Lessons for Peru
- Professional civil service essential for implementation
- Geographic advantages must be strategically leveraged
- Government procurement can bootstrap technology sectors
- International talent attraction accelerates development
